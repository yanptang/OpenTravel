# Iceland Ring Road Planning Notes

Iceland self-drive itineraries work best when each day has one clear geographic focus. Avoid mixing distant peninsulas, glacier zones, and long detours into the same day.

For arrival routing, make the transfer chain explicit: airport arrival, rental car pickup, drive into Reykjavik or the Reykjanes area, then only a light first-day activity.

Whale watching is easiest to explain as a destination-tied activity near a known harbor town such as Reykjavik, Husavik, or Akureyri. The itinerary should name the departure harbor and the return location.

Glacier hiking usually belongs with a south coast or Vatnajokull area day. It should not appear on a city day unless the transport leg clearly explains how the traveler gets to the glacier base.

Ring Road pacing feels more believable when hotel locations move logically around the route rather than jumping back and forth. Keep overnight cities aligned with the previous transport slot.

When describing transport on later days, the plan should say where the drive starts and where it ends, for example Reykjavik to Vik or Vik to Hofn. Generic titles like "drive to highlights" make route validation harder.
